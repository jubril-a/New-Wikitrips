TOEFL

Test of English as a Foreign LanguageTOEFL is a standardized test used to measure the English language proficiency of non-native speakers, particularly for academic purposes in English-speaking countries, such as the U.S., Canada, and others. It is often required for university admissions but can also be used for work and immigration purposes.

About the TOEFL

TOEFL (Test of English as a Foreign Language) is a standardized test that measures the English language ability of non-native speakers who wishes to enroll into English-speaking academic or professional institutions. TOEFL is divided into Reading, Writing, Listening and Speaking and the highest obtainable score is 120, with each section worth 30 marks each.
